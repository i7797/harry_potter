"Harry Potter" is a globally acclaimed fantasy novel series written by British author J.K. Rowling. The narrative revolves around a young wizard, Harry Potter, who discovers his magical heritage on his eleventh birthday. As he embarks on his journey at Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, Harry, accompanied by his loyal friends Hermione Granger and Ron Weasley, unravels the mysteries of his past and confronts the dark forces threatening the wizarding world. The series encompasses seven books, each detailing a year of Harry's education at Hogwarts and his ongoing battle against the dark wizard Lord Voldemort. Filled with captivating characters, magical creatures, and a richly detailed magical world, "Harry Potter" has captured the imaginations of readers of all ages, addressing themes of friendship, bravery, and the eternal struggle between good and evil. Rowling's storytelling prowess and the series' universal appeal have transformed it into a cultural phenomenon, leaving an indelible mark on literature and popular culture.
